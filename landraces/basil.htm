Basil is a virtual powerhouse of terpene action, including linalool, cineole, eugenol, sabinene, myrcene, and limonene.

Basil Varieties

Rama tulsi Basil

Indian 'Holy' Basil #1 aka 'Rama tulsi' is the most common form of Indian 'Holy' Basil.

The 'Rama tulsi' variety of basil forms a dome of small, bluish-green Terpene filled leaves on shrubs and buses about a 1/2 meter tall.

The leaves of the 'Rama tulsi' basil have a strong camphor scent while emanating simultaneously strong notes of cinnamon.

Krishna tulsi Basil

Indian 'Holy' Basil #2 aka 'Krishna tulsi' basil has a need for waves of strong summer heat to thrive.

As a result, the 'Krishna tulsi' variety has smaller leaves than the cooler, wetter weather 'Rama tulsi' variety described above.

'Krishna tulsi' basil is also distinguished by its reddish-purple stems.

More peppery than the soft camphor and cinnamon smelling cousin 'Rama tulsi', the 'Krishna tulsi' basil variety offers a greater kick for the crock pot.

Siam Queen Basil

Siam Queen ( Thai Basil ) has a wonderful anise flavor that pairs well with coconut milk. As anise and pumpkin are two flavors native to Thai cooking, the addition of Thai basil to a coconut milk base when prepared is quite distinctive. The Thai basil plants grow tall, almost a meter with the crown of the plant spreading about half its length in width. Colors of the Thai basil are similar to that of the Mexican basil ( see below ), but the Thai basil produces more leaves.

Purple Opal Basil

Purple Opal Basil ( Italian Basil ) has purple leaves and dark purple stems. As Italian basil is grown for its ornamental color as well as its flavor, the lustrous purple leaves make a nice addition to a salad of light greens. All basil varieties are sensitive to frost, none more so than the Purple Opal variety, though Italian in origin, nevertheless with roots deep in the tropics, a finicky breed to grow in containers that overnite outside during December's southwest cold dips towards the winter solstice.

The solution? Counter-intuitively water less and cover your Purple Opal Basil with sheets of nighttime plastic, stripping the cover when the sun starts to climb towards the noon day apex.

Lemon Basil

Lemon Basil is another favorite breed in the marketplace. Her basil plants have a pronounced degree of the terpene Limonene. Sow Lemon basil liberally after all risk of night time and early morning frost is over. You may notice the diminutive size of her leaves, smaller than most of the other varieties of basil. Add the fresh Lemon basil leaves and flowers to soups, fish, and seafood dishes for a terpene packed broth of potent medicine.

Cinnamon Basil

Cinnamon ( Mexican Basil ) is a favorite in most curry dishes. The leaves of the Mexican basil are half the size of the very green Genovese 'sweet' variety. If your chef is after a pronounced peppery - cinnamon flavor for your kitchen, Mexican basil is your call. A kaleidoscope of colors stem from the Mexican basil, from flower bracts of pink to rich, deep purple offerings, the Mexican basil is a striking addition to any edible landscape.
