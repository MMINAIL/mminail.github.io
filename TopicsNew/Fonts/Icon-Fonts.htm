A) When calculating the height and width of all future glyphs, an 'em' of '1em' corresponds to One-times X The initial letter's font-box vertical side. An 'em' of '2em' would simply be double the measurement of one vertical side a square box drawn around the initial letter (glyph) of the font-set. So, 'em' is a relative measurement of an actual measurement of one vertical side a square box drawn around the initial letter (glyph) of the font-set. These are called the 'boundaries' of the glyph.
