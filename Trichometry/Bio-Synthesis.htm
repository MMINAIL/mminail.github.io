Cannabinoid and Terpene Biosynthesis in Cannabis

Per Booth & Bohlmann, 2019 (University of British Columbia) in Terpenes in Cannabis sativa from Plant Genome to Humans the majority of terpenes found in Cannabis are simple hydrocarbons that are the direct products of Terpene synthase (TPS) enzymes.

Building Blocks

The genus Cannabaceae encompasses a very large pool of genetic diversity within the Rosales family of flowering plants and trees. As a result, very few of the modern strains resulting from breeding crosses within the Cannabis sativa L. species are homozygous. The reader may, therefore, expect a high level of heterozygosity and genetic admixture when studying the Cannabis plant empirically.

Building Blocks

Schematic of Terpene and Cannabinoid Biosynthesis in Cannabis from Booth & Bohlmann, 2019.

Follow along with the above diagram ...

  1. Two, five-carbon, or 5-Carbon (5-C) isoprenoid type biological building blocks are condensed to form Geranyl diphosphate (GPP, Carbon 10) or Farnesyl diphosphate (FPP, Carbon 15) in unequal amounts.

Note. The two, five-carbon, or 5-Carbon (5-C) isoprenoid type biological building blocks are:

  • isopententyl diphosphate (IPP), and
  • dimethylallyl diphosphate (DMAPP)
  1. Geranyl diphosphate (GPP) or Farnesyl diphosphate (FPP) in unequal amounts are then converted by Terpene synthases (TPS) into several types of terpenes.
  1. Further, aromatic prenyltransferases (aPTs) then condense olivetolic acid along with whatever Geranyl diphosphate (GPP) remains from the above Terpene synthesis to form Cannabigerolic acid (CBGA) which is then cyclized by several Cannabinoid synthases to produce the Cannabinoids of the plant.
  1. For example, Tetra-hydro-cannabinolic acid synthase (THCAS) produces carboxylated Tetra-hydro-cannabinolic acid (THCA).
  1. Then, when a molecule of Tetra-hydro-cannabinolic acid (THCA) is heated or smoked as part of your plant matter ritual, a carbon atom is de-carboxylated from the molecule to form Delta-9 Tetra-hydro-cannabinol (THC), the main ingredient of your "highness" along with its accompanying entourage of Terpenes and other Cannabinoids.
  1. Cannabinoids produced by the plant are:
  1. Canna-bi-gerolic acid (C1),
  2. Canna-bi-chromenic acid (C2),
  3. Canna-bi-diolic acid (C3), and
  4. Tetra-hydro-cannabinolic acid (C4).
