Home Brew'd Terms

In Homebrew, a 'Formula' is a package definition written in Ruby otherwise commonly known as a binary archive.

Whereas, a 'Bottle' in Homebrew is defined as a pre-built 'Keg'.

As an option to building from source, the 'Bottle', or pre-compiled binary package may simply be un-zip'd and allowed to propagate through the system at default.

Whereas, the 'Keg' is defined as the installation 'pre-fix' of a 'Formula'.

All 'Keg'(s), by the way, are installed in the 'Cellar' subdirectory of the Homebrew /usr/local/ pathway.

Interestingly, as anathema to Homebrew as the primary /opt/local/ pathway used by Macports is, Homebrew places its symbolic links in the /opt/ subdirectory of the /usr/local/ pathway defining the procedure as the 'opt prefix'!

Finally, 'Tap'(s) in Homebrew are Git repos house'd under the main /usr/local/Homebrew/Library/ thread.
