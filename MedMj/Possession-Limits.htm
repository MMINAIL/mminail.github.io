Missouri Medical Marijuana Possession Limits

What is the Missouri Medical Marijuana #MedMj Possession Limit For Qualified Patients?

Under the newly published guidelines by the Missouri Dept of Health and Human Services (DHSS), four (4) ounces is considered a thirty (30) day supply of Cannabis flowers for a Qualified Patient (QP) in Missouri.

Now, there are twenty-eight (28) grams of material in a dry-weight ounce of Cannabis flowers.

And, if the wholesale price of a gram of Cannabis flowers hovers around $5 per gram, then what is the estimated value of a thirty (30) day stash in Missouri?

Let's do the math ...

Four (4) ounces X 28 grams per ounce = 112 grams

112 grams X $5 USD per gram = $560 market value

Therefore, the expected value of a thirty (30) day stash of Cannabis flowers in Missouri is $560 USD.

Wow! That's WAY more than enough to make sure your thirty (30) day stash of Cannabis flowers remains in a safe place!

In fact, under the guidelines promulgated by the Missouri Dept of Health and Human Services (DHSS), should ANY of your supply becomes diverted for whatever reason other than supplying a Qualified Patient with his or her medicine, the prohibited act must be reported to the DHSS within three (3) days.

What is the monthly 'Carrryforward' for Care Providers and Qualified Patients?

If the State of Missouri allows the purchase of up to a thirty (30) day supply of Cannabis flowers, or four (4) ounces from a Missouri Registered Dispensary (MRD) every thirty (30) days, then what type of inventory 'Carrryforward' is allotted each Care Provider and/or Qualified Patient?

Answer: Well, even though you as a Qualified Patient may only be allowed to purchase a limit of up to a thirty (30) day supply, or four (4) ounces of Cannabis flowers from your local or remote Missouri Registered Dispensary (MRD) every thirty (30) days ...

The actual POSSESSION limit in Missouri has been set a little bit higher to accommodate up to a sixty (60) days supply, or eight (8) ounces of Cannabis flowers per Care Provider or Qualified Patient.

In other words, if you choose to "Grow Your Own", or GYO your thirty (30) day supply of Cannabis flowers, then the law as written allows you to stash a ninety (90) days supply, or twelve (12) ounces of Cannabis flowers per Care Provider or Qualified Patient!

Say what?

That's correct!

If all you are doing is buying your bud from a Missouri Registered Dispensary (MRD) every thirty (30) days, then you can be caught with a stash of up to a (60) days supply, or eight (8) ounces of Cannabis flower by dry-weight.

If, on the other hand you become, and your author highly recommends that you do become ... a Cannabis flower "indoor farmer",

Then you can be caught with a stash of up to a ninety (90) days supply, or a total of twelve (12) ounces!

Anything more, and the authorities will just assume you are trafficking illegally.

However, there is one loop hole in the current Missouri physician-based regime that may allow you as a Qualified Patient to possess or purchase more than the standard thirty (30) day supply of Cannabis flowers, or four (4) ounces.

Because the current regime under the petition titled 'Amendment 2' has now been etched into the constitution of the State of Missouri as Amendment XIV, if two or more physicians disagree on what your thirty (30) day supply of Cannabis flowers should be as measured in ounces, then the state will accept the lower of the two limits.

That's right ... one physician's decision on your condition as a Qualified Patient as it relates to your thirty (30) day supply of Cannabis flowers could be based on the Missouri State benchmark of four (4) ounces.

However, another physician, if you have taken the trouble to get a second opinion, may decide that you may indeed need a greater than the benchmark thirty (30) days supply of Cannabis flowers to alleviate your pain, aid in your nausea, etc.

For example, (10) ounces!

But, here's the catch.

If two or more certified physicians disagree on what that higher limit should be, then the DHSS will only accept the "lower" amount.

In other words, the DHSS will conservatively "round down" instead of "rounding up" which may not be the best avenue for the patient.

In this case, when applying for your Qualified Patient ID Card, or if you are updating a current record for a Qualified Patient at the DHSS, only submit the recommendation with the higher value.
